Help & Guide

Core Concepts
Cadence
How many days between check-ins. Set it once per contact and every time you log an interaction the Next Reminder automatically moves forward by that many days — no manual date picking needed.
Next Reminder
The date you want to reach out again. Contacts past this date are highlighted in your list — yellow (1–7 days late), orange (8–28 days), red (29+ days).
Logging an Interaction
Click the ✓ button on any row to record that you spoke today. This stamps Last Contacted and advances Next Reminder based on your cadence. Open the detail panel to attach a note to the log entry.
Views & Filters
Filters
Use the All / Due tabs to narrow your list. Due shows everyone whose reminder date has passed. Use tags to organise contacts by type (e.g. VC, Founder, Advisor).
Sorting
Click any column header (Name, Last Contacted, Next Reminder) to sort by that column. Click again to reverse the order.
Recently Added strip
The mini-cards above the controls show your 5 most recently added contacts. Click any to open their detail panel.
Import & Export
Export
Downloads all contacts as a CSV. You can also select specific contacts with the checkboxes and use Export Selected from the bulk action bar.
Import
Upload a CSV with columns: name, email, phone, linkedin, tags, notes, last_contacted, next_contact_reminder, cadence_days. Download the template for a ready-to-fill example.
To-Do Page
Switching pages
Use the Contacts / To-Do toggle in the header to switch between the two pages.
CRM sync
Contacts with an overdue reminder automatically appear as tasks in the To Do column, marked with a CRM badge. Logging an interaction or snoozing from the Contacts page will remove the task. Deleting or archiving a CRM task snoozes the contact for 1 week.
Drag & drop
Drag cards between columns to update their status, or drag within a column to reorder them. The order is saved automatically.
Archive column
Hover over a column header to reveal the Archive button, which deletes all tasks in that column at once.
Your Data
Where is it stored?
Everything lives in database.json in the project folder on your Mac. It never leaves your machine. The file is excluded from git so it won't be accidentally pushed to GitHub.
